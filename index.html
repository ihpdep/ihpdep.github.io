About Me

I am a final-year Ph.D. candidate at Zhejiang University, and my Ph.D. supervisor is Prof. Jianke Zhu. In 2018, I was working with Prof. Michael Yip and visiting at the University of California, San Diego. Since 2017, I've been working as a part-time researcher at Alibaba-Zhejiang University Joint Institute of Frontier Technologies. Besides, I received my Bachelor's degree in Software Engineering from East China Normal University (2007-2011) and spent some years in video game industries at Virtuos (2010-2012) before my Master's degree in the College of Computer Science from Zhejiang University (2013-2016).

Research Interest

My research interests are in the areas of Computer Vision and Machine Learning and, particularly, in methods of extracting meaningful information and structural data from videos and raw streaming sources. I am also very interested in finding and defining temporal information between video frames to provide visual perception for cross-disciplinary researches. Currently, my focus is on model-free single object tracking and 3D reconstruction/fusion.
